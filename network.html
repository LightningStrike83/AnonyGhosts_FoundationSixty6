Our Network

Foundation Sixty6 is comprised of everyone that strives to make a difference and connect people with the help that they need.

We are determined to continue expanding our network, expanding our list of counsellors, physicians, doctors, volunteers that care about mental health.

Why Become Part Of Our Network?

Foundation Sixty6 is built on the heart and soul of people who care about mental health and want to provide a safe and secure future for youths and their families. We are the people who want to make a difference in the world, because the cost of inaction is too great.

  • As of 2019, about 4000 Canadians die from suicide each year.
  • 14% of high-school students have seriously contemplated suicide
  • First Nations Youth die by suicide 6 times more often than non-Indigineous youth
  • Youths are resorting to self-diagnosis, and often times they are misinformed and cannot properly help themselves
  • Opportunities for stability disappear due to misinformation or misconceptions

Here in Ontario, we are facing major gaps that impact and impair those seeking mental health services.

  • As a society, we are facing a mental health epidemic, especially post-COVID
  • There is a major gap in Ontarioan healthcare, and finding help is more difficult than it should be
  • There is an inadequate number of doctors and healthcare providers who can provide those services as well
  • Struggling youth without substance use disorder (SUD) are deprioritized
  • When budget decisions are made, mental health is not a societal/political priority

We at Foundation Sixty6 want to help bridge that gap. We want to expand our network and provide support to struggling youths. We aim to make a difference in the world, and we encourage you to support and join our cause.

Our Goals

We are always looking to expand our network, but we also have a plan of action on how to use our network.

  • Build a network of healthcare providers: We want to connect patients to HCPs who are willing to support afforable access to mental health care.
  • Establish a Board of Governance: We want to create a board that serves the people and make our foundation known.
  • Build a network of companies and employers: We want to connect youths to employers who understand the struggles and are willing to provide flexible employment opportunities
  • Identify fundraising initiatives: We want to have a network that can provide sustainability and long-term support for Foundation Sixty6.
